## Administrative Closing Formulas

### KU-RO — "Total"

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Sequence | KU-RO |
| Proposed meaning | "total" (accounting grand total) |
| Confidence | High |
| Evidence tier | Tier 3 (Formulaic) |
| Corpus occurrences | 39 bigram occurrences |
| Basis | Positional analysis: KU-RO consistently appears at the end of accounting sequences after commodity+quantity pairs, in the same structural position as Linear B *to-so* ("so much / total") |
| Cross-site attestation | Haghia Triada (dominant), Khania, Phaistos, and others |
| First documented | Long-established in scholarship (Godart, Olivier, et al.) |

**Structural pattern:**
```
[entity A] [logogram] [number]
[entity B] [logogram] [number]
[entity C] [logogram] [number]
KU-RO [total number]
```

## Suffix Differentiation Findings (Tier 5, Preliminary — Added 2026-03-18)

Corpus-scale suffix × logogram co-occurrence analysis (2026-03-18) found the following distributional patterns. All interpretations are Tier 5 hypotheses — the distributional facts are Tier 1.

| Suffix | Records | Sites | KU-RO co-occ | KI-RO co-occ | Strongest co-occ | Preliminary interpretation |
|--------|---------|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| -RE | 51 | 11 | 21.6% | 15.7% | KU-RO, KI-RO | Possible agent/participant case in transactions |
| -JA | 58 | 17 | 3.4% | **0.0%** | GRA, VIR | Non-transactional noun marker; absent from deficit records |
| -TI | 40 | 13 | 17.5% | 5.0% | VIR (persons) | Possible personal name or occupational suffix |
| -TE | 52 | 20 | 3.8% | 5.8% | GRA, CYP | General vocabulary; most geographically widespread |

The zero KI-RO co-occurrence for -JA (58 records, 17 sites) is the most robust finding: -JA words appear in commodity-focused records but never in deficit-accounting records. This is consistent with -JA being a count-noun suffix or commodity category marker, not a case marker for transactional participants.

## JA-SA-SA-RA Root Analysis (Added 2026-03-18, Attempt #4)

The divine name root **JA-SA-SA-RA** is confirmed by the attestion of three inflected forms:

| Form | Count | Inflection |
|------|-------|-----------|
| JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | 7 | -ME suffix |
| JA-SA-SA-RA | 1 | bare root |
| JA-SA-SA-RA-MA | 1 | -MA suffix |

**Conclusion (Tier 3, Medium confidence):** -ME is a grammatical suffix detachable from the divine name root JA-SA-SA-RA. The bare form `JA-SA-SA-RA` is the absolute/dictionary form. The existence of `JA-SA-SA-RA-MA` suggests at minimum two inflectional paradigm positions for this name.

**Hypothesis (Tier 5, Low confidence):** -ME may be a dedicatory/vocative/divine case suffix that appears specifically when invoking the deity directly on a libation vessel. -MA may mark a different grammatical relationship (genitive? oblique?).

## A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA Formula (Added 2026-03-18, Attempt #4)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Occurrences | 11 |
| Support type | Stone vessel (100%) |
| Sites | Syme (5), Iouktas (3), Kophinas (1), Palaikastro (1), Troullos (1) |
| Position | Initial (10/11 inscriptions) |
| Evidence tier | Tier 3 (Formulaic) |
| Confidence | High (ritual header function) |

**Function:** This formula functions as a **ritual inscription header** — it appears at the start of ritual inscriptions and is followed by varying companion formulas at different sites.

**Variants (likely scribal):**
- `A-NA-TI-*301-WA-JA` — N instead of T in slot 2
- `A-TA-I-*301-WA-E` — -E instead of -JA at end
- `JA-TA-I-*301-U-JA` — JA- prefix; -U- in *301 slot
- `TA-NA-I-*301-U-TI-NU` — extended coda variant

**Note on *301 duality:** Sign *301 has TWO distinct uses:
1. In `A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA` (ritual formula): *301 is part of a phonetic/logographic ritual header
2. As standalone `*301` on HT nodules: 230/238 occurrences at Haghia Triada, administrative nodules only — likely an administrative classifier or heading sign unrelated to the ritual use
