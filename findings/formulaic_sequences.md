# Confirmed Formulaic Sequences

This file contains only high-confidence findings about recurring sequences in Linear A. Do not add speculative entries here — those belong in `hypotheses/`. Promote findings here only after cross-site validation.

## Administrative Closing Formulas

### KU-RO — "Total"

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Sequence | KU-RO |
| Proposed meaning | "total" (accounting grand total) |
| Confidence | High |
| Evidence tier | Tier 3 (Formulaic) |
| Corpus occurrences | 39 bigram occurrences |
| Basis | Positional analysis: KU-RO consistently appears at the end of accounting sequences after commodity+quantity pairs, in the same structural position as Linear B *to-so* ("so much / total") |
| Cross-site attestation | Haghia Triada (dominant), Khania, Phaistos, and others |
| First documented | Long-established in scholarship (Godart, Olivier, et al.) |

**Structural pattern:**
```
[entity A] [logogram] [number]
[entity B] [logogram] [number]
[entity C] [logogram] [number]
KU-RO [total number]
```

### KI-RO — "Owed / Deficit"

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Sequence | KI-RO |
| Proposed meaning | "owed" or "deficit" (accounting shortfall) |
| Confidence | High |
| Evidence tier | Tier 3 (Formulaic) |
| Corpus occurrences | ~17 bigram occurrences |
| Basis | Positional analysis: KI-RO appears in deficit/outstanding balance contexts, contrasted with KU-RO (total received/allocated). Parallel to Linear B accounting conventions for outstanding debts. |
| Cross-site attestation | Haghia Triada primarily; attested at other palatial sites |
| First documented | Established in scholarship |

**Structural pattern:**
```
[total] KU-RO [number]
[deficit/owed] KI-RO [number]
```

## Word-Final Suffix Distribution

The following syllables are the six most frequent word-final signs in the corpus. Their distribution is far from random, strongly suggesting grammatical suffixes (case markers, number markers, or tense/aspect markers in an agglutinative system).

| Suffix | Word-final occurrences | Notes |
|--------|----------------------|-------|
| -RO | 78 | Appears in KI-RO and many other tokens; possible case or functional marker |
| -JA | 65 | Common across administrative and non-administrative contexts |
| -RE | 58 | Possibly a different grammatical category from -RO despite phonological similarity |
| -TE | 56 | May be a personal name suffix or grammatical case marker |
| -NA | 54 | Possibly genitive or locative; cf. Hurrian locative -na |
| -TI | 49 | Common across multiple support types |

**Note:** These are counts from the corpus distributional analysis. The grammatical interpretation of each suffix is a Tier 5 hypothesis, not confirmed fact. The frequency data itself is Tier 1 (structural).

## KI-RO Site Distribution Note (Added 2026-03-18)

Corpus-scale analysis (2026-03-18) found KI-RO appearing at **Haghia Triada only** (16 of 16 occurrences). Despite being confirmed as a functional administrative term, its cross-site evidence is weak. It should not be treated as a multi-site anchor until further occurrences are identified or explained.

## Ritual Register Formulas (Candidate, Medium Confidence)

The following bigrams appear on non-administrative support types (stone vessels, libation tables) at multiple sites. They are distinct from administrative closing formulas and likely belong to a ritual register.

### `I-PI-NA-MA SI-RU-TE`

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Sequence | I-PI-NA-MA SI-RU-TE (two-word bigram in fixed order) |
| Proposed register | Ritual (libation/dedication formula) |
| Confidence | Medium |
| Evidence tier | Tier 3 (Formulaic) |
| Corpus occurrences | 5 bigram occurrences |
| Sites | Iouktas, Kophinas, Troullos, Vrysinas (all secondary cult sites, not palace archives) |
| Basis | Fixed word-order stability across 4 geographically distinct cult sites |
| First documented | 2026-03-18 (this analysis) |

**Note:** Meaning unknown. I-PI-NA-MA appears alone at other sites; SI-RU-TE is its consistent follow-on at cult sites. May be a ritual invocation, divine title pair, or dedicatory phrase.

## Suffix Differentiation Findings (Tier 5, Preliminary — Added 2026-03-18)

Corpus-scale suffix × logogram co-occurrence analysis (2026-03-18) found the following distributional patterns. All interpretations are Tier 5 hypotheses — the distributional facts are Tier 1.

| Suffix | Records | Sites | KU-RO co-occ | KI-RO co-occ | Strongest co-occ | Preliminary interpretation |
|--------|---------|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| -RE | 51 | 11 | 21.6% | 15.7% | KU-RO, KI-RO | Possible agent/participant case in transactions |
| -JA | 58 | 17 | 3.4% | **0.0%** | GRA, VIR | Non-transactional noun marker; absent from deficit records |
| -TI | 40 | 13 | 17.5% | 5.0% | VIR (persons) | Possible personal name or occupational suffix |
| -TE | 52 | 20 | 3.8% | 5.8% | GRA, CYP | General vocabulary; most geographically widespread |
| -NA | 50 | 12 | 12.0% | 6.0% | GRA, VIR | Possibly locative/genitive; cf. Hurrian -na |

The zero KI-RO co-occurrence for -JA (58 records, 17 sites) is the most robust finding: -JA words appear in commodity-focused records but never in deficit-accounting records. This is consistent with -JA being a count-noun suffix or commodity category marker, not a case marker for transactional participants.
